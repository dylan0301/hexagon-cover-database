# Vd1 / Vd2 Constraints

Status: Lemma target / used in proven CE0 package

A Vd1 triangle has one positive-length adjacent-ray intersection. A Vd2 triangle has two.

In the CE0 + Vd1/Vd2 half-skeleton proof, Vd2 is automatically handled by choosing either adjacent-ray intersection. The second intersection is an additional constraint, not extra freedom.

Target inequality form:

$$
\Phi_{\mathrm{Vd1},M_{i\pm1}}(a_i,b_i)\le0.
$$

The exact local geometry is developed in the CE0 proof package, especially the rhombus geometry and frontier perturbation files.
