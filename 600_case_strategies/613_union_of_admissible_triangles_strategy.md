# Union of Admissible Triangles Strategy

Status: Strategy

For fixed local boundary data \((a,b)\), define

\[
\mathcal T_i(a,b)=\{T_i:\text{local boundary data is }(a,b)\}.
\]

The relaxed region is

\[
U_i(a,b)=\bigcup_{T\in\mathcal T_i(a,b)}(T\cap H).
\]

If even these relaxed regions cannot cover a target, then actual triangles cannot cover it.

Questions:

- Is \(U_i(a,b)\) semialgebraic?
- Can it be described by few polynomial inequalities?
- Is the relaxation too weak?
