# AB-Set Package Index

Status: Reference

This package separates a rigorously proved strict-supercritical formula from a
larger empirical case catalog for the **ab-set**.

## Problem

Let $O=(0,0)$ be the origin, $B=(b,0)$ a point on the positive $x$-axis, and
$A=a (\cos120^\circ,\sin120^\circ)$, so that $\angle AOB=120^\circ$ (counterclockwise, $A$ in the
second quadrant), with $|AO|=a\ge0$ and $|BO|=b\ge0$. Let $C$ be the closed $120^\circ$ convex cone
generated by the rays $OB$ and $OA$.

> **The ab-set** is
> $$K(a,b)=\Bigl(\bigcup\{ T:\ T\ \text{a unit equilateral triangle with }\{A,O,B\}\subset T \}\Bigr)\cap C.$$

The goal is to describe $K(a,b)$ for **all** $a,b\ge0$: break the parameter quadrant into cases
given by polynomial inequalities, and for each case give the boundary of $K(a,b)$ as a list of
algebraic curves in counterclockwise order — with proof.

## The proposed general answer, in one paragraph

Write $\sigma=a+b$, $R=\sqrt{a^2+ab+b^2}=|AB|$, $h=\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2}$. A finite set fits in a unit
equilateral triangle of orientation $\varphi$ **iff** the sum of its three support values (in the
directions $\varphi,\varphi+120^\circ,\varphi+240^\circ$) is $\le h$ — a Viviani-type criterion.
This turns $K$ into a union of triangles over the admissible orientations, and its boundary is
traced by a **finite catalog** of curves:

* $K(a,b)=\varnothing$ exactly when $R>1$; and $K$ is a single triangle $\cap C$ when $R=1$;
* otherwise the boundary consists of: the two **cone edges**; **circles of radius 1** centered at
  $A$ and at $B$; four **limaçon quartics** $E_{AO},E_{BO},E_{BA},E_{AB}$ (the locus of the free
  vertex $x$ as a unit triangle pivots with one of $A,O,B$ on a side through $x$ and another on the
  opposite side); six **flush lines** ($L_A,L_B,M_A,M_B,\lambda_A,\lambda_B$, from a triangle side
  lying along $OA$ or $OB$); and **extreme-orientation triangle-side segments**.

The cases are organized into four **bands** by the topology of the admissible-orientation set
$\Phi=\{\varphi:F(\varphi)\le h\}$:

| band | inequalities | admissible orientations |
|---|---|---|
| I   | $4(a+b)^2\le3$ | all of them |
| II  | $4(a+b)^2>3,\ 4R^2\le3$ | one gap ("outer gap") |
| III | $4R^2>3,\ a+b\le1$ | two gaps ("outer" + "mid") |
| IV  | $a+b>1,\ R^2\le1$ | a single arc |

Within each band, finer structure is decided by explicit polynomial thresholds ($a=\tfrac12$,
$b=\tfrac12$, $a+b=\tfrac12$; flank cubics $w_\pm$; the flush-line quintic $\mathcal G$; the mid-dip
condition $(3+a)R^2=3$; and a resultant threshold $\mathcal E$), giving about twenty sub-cases in
total (plus mirror images under $a\leftrightarrow b$). The full case tables, with each boundary
written as an explicit counterclockwise list of the catalog curves, are in
[`20092_ab_set_case_catalog.md`](20092_ab_set_case_catalog.md).

## Reproducing the verification

```sh
python3 2009X_computation/verify_ab_set.py          # full run (region audit + corner identities + per-case table)
python3 2009X_computation/verify_ab_set.py quick    # faster, lower-resolution pass
python3 2009X_computation/derive_quartics.py        # print the quartic curve equations
python3 2009X_computation/derive_thresholds.py      # print the exact threshold/tangency polynomials
```

Requires `numpy` and `sympy`. The checker computes the boundary from first principles (no use of
the claimed answer) and checks that, for each sampled direction, the boundary point lies on the
curve claimed for that arc and that the arcs appear in the stated counterclockwise order.

## Status

The strict branch $a+b>1$, $R^2<1$ candidate is isolated in
[`20091_ab_union_curve_a_plus_b_gt_1.md`](20091_ab_union_curve_a_plus_b_gt_1.md),
but its support-pattern partition is incomplete and one former intermediate
equivalence has an exact counterexample.  The larger Bands I--III catalog is
also not yet a proof: its advertised
curve-order, domination, pruning, and sign-region claims are checked only on
finite numerical samples.  The exact missing obligations are recorded at the
start of [`20093_ab_set_proofs.md`](20093_ab_set_proofs.md).  The experiments
remain useful evidence, summarized in
[`20094_ab_set_verification.md`](20094_ab_set_verification.md), but they do not
upgrade the general catalog above `Empirical`.
