# The hexagonal hole set cannot be covered by a unit equilateral triangle

This file proves the theorem stated in `prompts/hole-set-noncover.md`, in the notation of
`ab_set.md` / `ab_set_proofs.md`. The proof is a branch-and-bound case analysis whose leaves are
discharged by exact positivity certificates over $\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)$; the certificate data lives in
`hole_certificates_corner.py` and `hole_certificates_bulk.py` (generated by `gen_corner.py`,
`gen_bulk.py`), and the standalone verifier `verify_hole_noncover.py` re-checks every certificate
from scratch.

Throughout, $h=\tfrac{\sqrt3}2$, $u(\theta)=(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)$, and $K(a,b)$ is the ab-set of
`ab_set.md` (apex $O$, ray $OB$ of length $b$ along $0^\circ$, ray $OA$ of length $a$ along
$120^\circ$, cone $C$ spanned by them).

## 1. Setup and statement

Let $H$ be the regular hexagon with vertices $V_k=u(60^\circ k)$, $k=0,\dots,5$ (indices mod 6).
On edge $V_iV_{i+1}$ mark the point $p_i$ with $|V_ip_i|=a_i$; write $b_i=1-a_{i-1}$, so
$q_i:=p_{i-1}$ has $|V_iq_i|=b_i$. At each vertex $V_i$ place the ab-set
$$K_i \;=\; K(a_i,b_i)\ \text{in the frame with apex } V_i,\ B\text{-ray toward }V_{i-1},\
A\text{-ray toward }V_{i+1}.$$
Because the interior angle of $H$ is $120^\circ$, $K_i$ occupies the corner wedge of $H$ at $V_i$.
The **hole set** is
$$S \;=\; H\setminus (K_0\cup\dots\cup K_5).$$

**Local frames.** The frame at $V_i$ is *orientation-reversing*: the catalog point $(X,Y)$ sits at
$$\Xi_i(X,Y)\;=\;V_i+X\,u(\beta_i)+Y\,u(\beta_i-90^\circ),\qquad \beta_i=240^\circ+60^\circ i .$$
A catalog direction $u(\psi)$ maps to the global direction $u(\beta_i-\psi)$.

**Standing assumptions.** (i) $R_i^2=a_i^2+a_ib_i+b_i^2\le1$ for all $i$; (ii) $a_0+b_0>1$ and
$a_i+b_i<1$ for $i=1,\dots,5$. As derived in the prompt, (ii) is exactly the strict chain
$$a_0>a_1>\dots>a_5 ,$$
and (i) is automatic except at $i=0$, where it reads $R_0^2=a_0^2+a_0(1-a_5)+(1-a_5)^2\le1$.
Note $a_5>0$ is forced: $a_5=0$ would give $b_0=1$ and $R_0^2=a_0^2+a_0+1>1$.

**Theorem.** Under the standing assumptions, for every orientation $\varphi$,
$$F_S(\varphi)\;=\;\sum_{k=0}^2 h_S\big(u(\varphi+120^\circ k)\big)\;>\;h .$$
Consequently (fitting criterion, `ab_set_proofs.md` Lemma 1.1) $S$ is contained in no unit
equilateral triangle. Quantitatively the certificates give
$F_S(\varphi)\ge h+\varepsilon\,\pi(a_5,\,a_0)$ for an explicit positive polynomial $\pi$ and
rational $\varepsilon>0$ on each branch, and $\pi\to0$ exactly in the two corner limits of §8.

## 2. Reductions

**Lemma 2.1 (monotonicity).** If $a\le a'$ and $b\le b'$ then $K(a',b')\subseteq K(a,b)$.

*Proof.* $A=a\,u(120^\circ)$ lies on the segment $[O,A']$, so any (convex) triangle containing
$\{A',O,B'\}$ contains $\{A,O,B\}$; the defining triangle family of $K(a',b')$ is a subfamily of
that of $K(a,b)$, and the cone is the same. $\square$

**Lemma 2.2 (mirror symmetry).** Let $\rho$ be the reflection across the $x$-axis (the line
$OV_0$). If $(a_0,\dots,a_5)$ satisfies the standing assumptions, so does
$a'=(1-a_5,1-a_4,\dots,1-a_0)$, and $\rho(S(a))=S(a')$. Hence
$F_{S(a')}(\varphi)=F_{S(a)}(120^\circ-\varphi)$, and it suffices to prove the theorem for
$a_5+a_0\le1$.

*Proof.* $\rho$ maps $H$ to $H$ with $V_i\mapsto V_{-i}$ and maps the edge point $p_i$ (at distance
$a_i$ from $V_i$) to the point at distance $1-a_i$ from $V_{-i-1}$ on edge $[V_{-i-1},V_{-i}]$;
reading off the new edge parameters gives $a'_j=1-a_{5-j}$. Since reflections map unit triangles
to unit triangles and the vertex wedges to vertex wedges (with the $A/B$ roles swapped, matching
the orientation-reversing frames), $\rho(K_i(a))=K_{-i}(a')$, so the holes correspond. The chain
$a'_0>\dots>a'_5$ and $R'^2_0=R_0^2$ are immediate. For $F$: $h_{\rho S}(u(\psi))=
h_S(u(-\psi))$ and the normal triple at $\varphi$ reflects to the triple at
$-\varphi\equiv120^\circ-\varphi \pmod{120^\circ}$. Finally $a_5+a_0\le 1$ or
$a'_5+a'_0=2-(a_0+a_5)\le1$. $\square$

**Reduction to two parameters.** Set
$$s=a_5,\qquad t=a_0,\qquad
\widetilde K_0=K(t,\,1-s)\ \text{at}\ V_0,\qquad
\widetilde K_i=K(s,\,1-t)\ \text{at}\ V_i\ (i=1,\dots,5),$$
$$\widetilde S(s,t)\;=\;H\setminus\big(\widetilde K_0\cup\dots\cup\widetilde K_5\big).$$

**Lemma 2.3 (reduction).** Under the standing assumptions, $S\supseteq\widetilde S(s,t)$, and
$(s,t)$ lies in
$$T\;=\;\Big\{(s,t):\ 0<s\le t,\ \ R_0^2(s,t):=t^2+t(1-s)+(1-s)^2\le1\Big\},$$
with $t>s$ strictly. (By Lemma 2.2 we may and do also assume $s+t\le1$.)

*Proof.* For $i\ge1$: $a_i\ge a_5=s$ and $b_i=1-a_{i-1}\ge1-a_0=1-t$, so by Lemma 2.1
$K_i\subseteq K(s,1-t)=\widetilde K_i$ (same frame). For $i=0$: $(a_0,b_0)=(t,1-s)$ exactly, so
$K_0=\widetilde K_0$. Removing larger sets leaves a smaller difference, whence
$S\supseteq\widetilde S$. The constraint set is the standing assumption at $i=0$. $\square$

So it suffices to exhibit, for each $(s,t)\in T$ with $s+t\le 1$ and each $\varphi$, three points
$r_1,r_2,r_3\in\widetilde S(s,t)$ with
$$L(\varphi)\;=\;\big\langle r_1,u(\varphi)\big\rangle+\big\langle r_2,u(\varphi+120^\circ)\big\rangle
+\big\langle r_3,u(\varphi+240^\circ)\big\rangle\;>\;h,$$
since $r_k\in\widetilde S\subseteq S$ gives $F_S(\varphi)\ge L(\varphi)$. Note the equivalent form
$L(\varphi)=\langle w,u(\varphi)\rangle$ with
$w=r_1+R_{-120^\circ}r_2+R_{-240^\circ}r_3$.

**Region remark.** $T$ pinches at the origin: $1-R_0^2 = (2s-t)-s^2+st-t^2$, so $R_0\le1$ forces
$t\le 2s-s^2+st-t^2\le 2s$; in particular $2s-t\ge s^2-st+t^2\ge0$ on $T$ — this inequality (the
generator `dg` below) is where the existence constraint $R_0\le1$ enters the leaf certificates.
On the admissible set $s>0$ and $t>s$; the closed faces $R_0=1$ and $s=1/32$ (an internal split)
are included in the branches, while $t=s$ and $s=0$ are excluded boundary faces (there
$a_0+b_0>1$, resp. admissibility, fails).

## 3. Certificate primitives

All leaf claims reduce to the following three elementary lemmas plus polynomial inequalities in
$(s,t)$ over $\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)$.

**Lemma 3.1 (arc lemma).** Let $d_1,d_2$ be nonzero vectors with $d_1\times d_2>0$ (positively
oriented, angle $<180^\circ$), and let $u$ range over the unit vectors in the closed convex cone
spanned by $d_1,d_2$. If $\langle w,d_k\rangle\ge (h+\epsilon)\,|d_k|$ for $k=1,2$ and some
$\epsilon\ge 0$, then $\langle w,u\rangle\ge h+\epsilon$ on the whole arc.

*Proof.* $u=(\alpha d_1+\beta d_2)/|\alpha d_1+\beta d_2|$ with $\alpha,\beta\ge0$, and
$\langle w,\alpha d_1+\beta d_2\rangle\ge(h+\epsilon)(\alpha|d_1|+\beta|d_2|)
\ge(h+\epsilon)\,|\alpha d_1+\beta d_2|$ by the triangle inequality. $\square$

In practice each endpoint condition is certified as the pair
$$\langle w,d\rangle\ \ge\ 0,\qquad \langle w,d\rangle^2-h^2\,|d|^2\ \ge\ (\text{strictness term})\ >\ 0 ,$$
which together give $\langle w,d\rangle>h|d|$.

**Lemma 3.2 (membership form; disk lemma).** $x\in K(a,b)$ iff $x\in C$ and
$\min_\varphi F_{\{A,O,B,x\}}(\varphi)\le h$ (Corollary 1.2 of `ab_set_proofs.md`). Consequently:

* (disk) if $|x-P|>1$ for some $P\in\{A,O,B\}$ then $x\notin K(a,b)$ — a unit triangle has
  diameter 1 (Lemma 3.1 of `ab_set_proofs.md`);
* (cone) if $x\notin C$ then $x\notin K(a,b)$.

**Lemma 3.3 (selection lemma).** For any $x$ and any *selection*
$\sigma=(\sigma_0,\sigma_1,\sigma_2)\in\{A,O,B,x\}^3$,
$$F_{\{A,O,B,x\}}(\varphi)\;\ge\;\Big\langle w_\sigma,\,u(\varphi)\Big\rangle,\qquad
w_\sigma=\sigma_0+R_{-120^\circ}\sigma_1+R_{-240^\circ}\sigma_2 ,$$
because each summand $\max_{p}\langle p,u(\varphi+120^\circ k)\rangle\ge\langle
\sigma_k,u(\varphi+120^\circ k)\rangle$. Hence if a family of arcs (Lemma 3.1) covering a full
$120^\circ$ period carries selections with $\langle w_\sigma,u\rangle>h$ on each arc, then
$\min_\varphi F_{\{A,O,B,x\}}>h$ and $x\notin K(a,b)$.

A point $x$ with $x\in H$ and $x\notin \widetilde K_j$ for all $j$ (each shown by Lemma 3.2 or 3.3
in the local frame of $\widetilde K_j$) lies in $\widetilde S(s,t)$ by definition. The theorem's
own bound $F_S\ge L$ uses arcs over the triangle-orientation circle in exactly the same format
(the fitting arcs of §5): a full period $[0^\circ,120^\circ]$ is covered because consecutive arcs
share endpoints, the first starts at $u(0^\circ)$ and the last ends at $u(120^\circ)$.

**Positivity certificates.** Every required inequality is a polynomial
$q\in\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)[s,t]$ (rational functions are cleared against denominators that are
themselves certified positive) to be shown $\ge0$ on a *leaf region*
$\{g_1\ge0,\dots,g_r\ge0\}$. The certificate is an exact Handelman-style identity
$$q\;=\;\sum_\alpha c_\alpha\, g_1^{\alpha_1}\cdots g_r^{\alpha_r},\qquad
c_\alpha\in\mathbb Q(\sqrt3),\ c_\alpha\ge0 ,$$
verified by literal expansion; nonnegativity of each $c_\alpha=p+q\sqrt3$ is decided exactly
(comparing $p^2$ with $3q^2$). Where strict positivity is needed on the open admissible set, the
certificate contains a designated term $c_\alpha s^{k}(t-s)^{m}$ with $c_\alpha>0$ (all other
terms being $\ge0$ on the region), so $q\ge c_\alpha s^k(t-s)^m>0$ whenever $s>0,\ t>s$. This is
the *equality-preserving structure*: every strict margin degenerates only via powers of $s$ and
$t-s$, i.e. only toward the excluded faces and the corner $s\to0$.

The corner-leaf generators are
$$g\in\Big\{\,s,\quad t-s,\quad 1-R_0^2,\quad \tfrac1{32}-s,\quad 2s-t,\quad
\pm(8s-5t)\,\Big\},$$
where $2s-t\ge0$ follows on $T$ from $2s-t=(1-R_0^2)+(s^2-st+t^2)$ and
$s^2-st+t^2=(s-\tfrac t2)^2+\tfrac34t^2\ge0$ (the verifier checks this identity), and $8s-5t\ge0$
resp. $5t-8s\ge0$ is the leaf split below.

## 4. The branch tree

```
admissible (a_0..a_5)                       [standing assumptions]
└─ reduce: (s,t) = (a_5,a_0) ∈ T            [Lemma 2.3]
   └─ WLOG s+t ≤ 1                          [Lemma 2.2, mirror]
      ├─ zone C: s ≤ 1/32
      │   ├─ leaf C-low : 8s-5t ≥ 0         [3 fitting arcs, 6 witnesses]
      │   └─ leaf C-high: 5t-8s ≥ 0         [3 fitting arcs, 6 witnesses]
      ├─ leaf C-ext: 1/32 ≤ s ≤ 1/16,
      │              s ≤ t ≤ (5/4)s         [C-low witnesses re-certified]
      └─ bulk: 1/32 ≤ s (≤ 1/2 automatic), t ≥ (5/4)s when s ≤ 1/16
          └─ recursive boxes [s1,s2]×[t1,t2] in two roots split at s=1/16
             (certified or disjoint)          [fixed supersets, constant
                                               witnesses and arcs]
```

The $\varphi$-circle at every leaf is split into arcs by *measuring-direction* cuts (inequalities
in $c=\cos\varphi,\ \sigma=\sin\varphi$), the only parameter splits being the catalog-style loci
$s=\tfrac1{32}$, $8s=5t$, $s=\tfrac1{16}$, $4t=5s$, $R_0^2=1$ and the box faces. Cut directions
need not be unit vectors (Lemma 3.1 is scale-free); parametric cuts are taken as *linear tilts*
$$e \;=\; u(\theta_0)\; -\; c\,\mu\, u(\theta_0+90^\circ),\qquad c\in\mathbb Q,\
\mu\in\{s,\ t,\ t-s\},$$
i.e. exact $\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)[s,t]$-vectors at angle $\theta_0-\arctan(c\mu)$, which keeps every
endpoint polynomial at low degree.

## 5. The corner leaves (zone C: $0<s\le\tfrac1{32}$)

This is the delicate zone containing the equality corner $(s,t)\to(0,0)$. In the limit,
$\widetilde K_i\to$ the sector triangle $\mathrm{conv}\{V_{i-1},V_i,O\}$ for $i=1,\dots,5$ while
$\widetilde K_0$ collapses to the segment $[V_0,V_5]$, so $\widetilde S\to$ the **unit sector
triangle $OV_0V_5$**, whose optimal orientation is $\varphi=90^\circ$ (edge normals
$90^\circ,210^\circ,330^\circ$) with $F=h$ exactly.

**Witnesses.** With $g:=t-s$ and $\kappa$ given by $\tan\frac\kappa2=\xi_\star$:

| name | definition | role (limit) |
|---|---|---|
| $W_0$ | $(0,0)$ | corner $O$ |
| $W_{V0b}$ | $V_0+t\,u(240^\circ)+2t\,u(150^\circ)$ | corner $V_0$ |
| $W_{V5}$ | $V_5+2t\,u(90^\circ)+\tfrac t2u(60^\circ)$ | corner $V_5$ |
| $W_{st1}$ | $\Xi_1\big(z(30^\circ-\kappa)+\varepsilon_\star u(60^\circ-\kappa)\big)$ | the near-$O$ *star* point on petal 1 |
| $W_{st3}$ | $R_{120^\circ}W_{st1}$ | star point on petal 3 |
| $W_{dip}$ | $\tfrac{A_0+B_0}2+\big(\tfrac4{15}(1-R_0^2)+\tfrac{s^2}{16}\big)\,m$ | the sliver's band-IV dip corner |

Here $z(\varphi)=e^{i(\varphi+30^\circ)}+\tfrac s{\sqrt3}e^{i150^\circ}(1+e^{2i\varphi})$ is the
$E_{AO}$ limaçon of petal 1 (catalog §1) in its local frame — the star witness sits exactly on the
quartic, at the rational-trigonometry parameter $\xi_\star$, then is pushed off the boundary by
$\varepsilon_\star$ along the outward direction; $A_0=V_0+t\,u(120^\circ)$ and
$B_0=V_0+(1-s)u(240^\circ)$ are the sliver's defining points, and
$m=\Xi_0'(\sqrt3\,t,\ t+2(1-s))$ (the un-normalized inward chord normal, $\Xi_0'$ = frame-0 linear
part). The leaf split tunes the star:

* **C-low** ($5t\le8s$) and **C-ext**: $\xi_\star=\tfrac{21}{40}g$,
  $\varepsilon_\star=\tfrac g{20}$ (i.e. $\kappa\approx1.05\,g$: the free arc of $E_{AO}$
  between the clip by petal 2 and the outer-gap end $W_+$ scales with $g$);
* **C-high** ($5t\ge8s$): $\xi_\star=\tfrac s3$, $\varepsilon_\star=\tfrac g{60}$
  (i.e. $\kappa\approx\tfrac23 s$: for large gap ratio the free arc saturates at the $s$-scale).

The third leaf **C-ext** ($\tfrac1{32}\le s\le\tfrac1{16}$, $t\le\tfrac54 s$) re-certifies the
C-low design on the diagonal wedge adjoining zone C; it exists because bulk boxes touching the
diagonal at small $s$ would need sliver supersets with $\sigma_0<1$ (band III instead of IV),
which destroys the fixed-hole margin.

$W_{dip}$ sits beyond the sliver's inner boundary at the chord midpoint: the dip corner
$s_A^{\mathrm{IV}}\cap s_B^{\mathrm{IV}}$ of the band-IV chain lies over the chord midpoint
($\mu=\tfrac12$ exactly, verified numerically to $10^{-12}$ and enforced by certificate) at chord
depth $\tfrac{\sqrt3}4(1-R_0^2)\,\chi(R_0)$, $\chi(1)=1$; the coefficient $\tfrac4{15}$ exceeds the
exact depth on zone C and the $\tfrac{s^2}{16}$ bump keeps $W_{dip}$ strictly outside
$\widetilde K_0$ on the closed face $R_0=1$ (where the sliver degenerates to the tray
$\mathrm{conv}\{A_0,V_0,B_0\}$).

**Validity.** Each witness is certified to lie in $H$ (six linear inequalities) and outside all six
sets. Selection certificates are built as shortest paths in a graph whose nodes are exact cut
directions (constants, linear tilts, and the *chord-normal family*
$m\pm c\,(1-R_0^2)\,m^\perp$ with $m=(\sqrt3a,\,a+2b)$ un-normalized, which tracks each set's
feasibility window exactly) and whose edges carry the selection with the best worst-sample margin
at both endpoints. When the required selection swaps across the leaf, the $s$-interval is
bisected and each piece certified separately (`sel`/`sel-tree` entries carry their intervals).
$W_{dip}$'s far-set disk certificates are assembled *structurally* from low-degree pieces
($q=|{\rm mid}-P|^2-1$, $\langle{\rm mid}-P,m\rangle$ bounded below, $\delta$ bounded above)
multiplied out into a single positive combination. The far pairs use the disk/cone lemma; the near pairs use selection certificates
(Lemma 3.3), whose arcs are listed in the data file; the two critical orientations of a selection
certificate always sit near the local $30^\circ$ and $90^\circ$ (the two edge-parallel
orientations of the segment $[O_{loc},x]$), which is where the arc cuts live. By exact rotational
congruence, $W_{st3}$-vs-petals $3,4,5$ reuse the $W_{st1}$-vs-petals $1,2,3$ certificates, and
$W_0$-vs-petal is a single certificate reused for all five petals (the center has the same local
coordinates $(\tfrac12,\tfrac{\sqrt3}2)$ in every frame).

**Fitting arcs.** With cuts $e_L=(c_Lt,\,1)$, $e_R=(-\tfrac52t,\,1)$ — directions
$90^\circ-\arctan(c_Lt)$ and $90^\circ+\arctan(\tfrac52 t)$, where $c_L=\tfrac34$ on C-low/C-high
and $c_L=1$ on C-ext:

| arc | range | triple $(r_1,r_2,r_3)$ | limit identity at $s\to0$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| $A$ | $[u(0^\circ),e_L]$ | $(W_{V0b},\,W_0,\,W_{V5})$ | $L\to\cos\varphi+\cos(\varphi-60^\circ)$, tight end at $90^\circ$ |
| $B$ | $[e_L,e_R]$ | $(W_{st1},\,W_{st3},\,W_{dip})$ | $L\to h$ **exactly** (the equality corner) |
| $D$ | $[e_R,u(120^\circ)]$ | $(W_{st1},\,W_{V5},\,W_{dip})$ | $L\to$ rising flank |

The central arc $B$ carries the equality: at $(s,t)\to0$, $W_{st1},W_{st3}\to O$ and
$W_{dip}\to$ the midpoint of $[V_0,V_5]$, and $L(90^\circ)\to0+0+h=h$. At first order the budget is
$$L(90^\circ)-h\;\approx\;\underbrace{2\Big(\tfrac{\sqrt3}2s-\tfrac\kappa2\Big)}_{\text{two star
supports}}\;-\;\underbrace{\tfrac{\sqrt3}2\,s}_{\text{dip retreat}}\;+\;O(\varepsilon_\star)+O(2),$$
positive precisely because $R_0^2\le1$ caps $t$ below $\approx2s$ — the existence constraint is
what wins the corner. All eight arc-endpoint inequalities (per leaf) carry strictness terms
$\varepsilon\,s^k(t-s)^m$; the tight ones vanish linearly in $s$ at the corner, exposing the
equality case exactly as required.

## 6. The bulk zone ($\tfrac1{32}\le s$, $s\le t\le\min(t_{\max}(s),1-s)$)

Note $s\le\tfrac12$ is automatic from $s\le t$ and $s+t\le1$. The zone is covered by a recursive
subdivision of the root box $[\tfrac1{32},\tfrac12]\times[\tfrac1{32},\tfrac23]$ into axis boxes.
A box is either **disjoint** from the region — certified by one of the exact conditions
$t_2\le s_1$ (below the diagonal), $t_1\ge1-s_1$ (beyond the mirror line), or
$R_0^2(s_2,t_1)\ge1$ (beyond the existence ellipse) — or **certified**:

* By Lemma 2.1, for $(s,t)$ in the box (feasible part), $\widetilde K_i\subseteq
  \widehat K_i:=K(s_1,\,1-t_2^{\mathrm{eff}})$ at $V_i$ ($i\ge1$) and $\widetilde K_0\subseteq
  \widehat K_0:=K(t_1,\,1-s_2)$, where $t_2^{\mathrm{eff}}\ge\min(t_2,\ t_{\max}(s_2),\ 1-s_1)$ is
  a stored rational with $R_0^2(s_2,t_2^{\mathrm{eff}})\ge1$ or $t_2^{\mathrm{eff}}=\min(t_2,1-s_1)$
  (so it really bounds $t$ on the feasible part: $t\le t_{\max}(s)\le t_{\max}(s_2)$ since
  $R_0^2$ decreases in $s$).
* Constant rational witnesses (found as support points of the fixed hole
  $H\setminus\bigcup\widehat K_j$, pulled inward) are certified in $H$ and outside every
  $\widehat K_j$ by the disk/cone/selection lemmas — all checks are sign evaluations of explicit
  $\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)$ numbers.
* Constant fitting arcs with witness triples cover $[0^\circ,120^\circ]$ with
  $\langle w,d\rangle>h|d|$ at all endpoints.

Since the fixed sets contain every $\widetilde K_j$ of the box, the constant witnesses lie in
$\widetilde S(s,t)$ for all $(s,t)$ in the box's feasible part, and the constant arc bounds give
$F>h$ there uniformly.

## 7. Verification

`verify_hole_noncover.py` re-derives everything from the definitions and checks, exactly over
$\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)$:

1. the tree covers the reduced region: C-low $\cup$ C-high $=$ zone C (the split faces are
   complementary), the bulk boxes tile the root box (exact bisection tree), disjoint boxes'
   reasons hold, and the root box contains the bulk region;
2. generator soundness: the `dg` $=2s-t$ derivation and box $t_2^{\mathrm{eff}}$ facts;
3. every Handelman identity: literal expansion equals the claimed polynomial, every coefficient
   is $\ge0$ (exact sign in $\mathbb Q(\sqrt3)$), strictness terms present where required;
   auxiliary square generators (`aux_sq`) are admitted only as literal squares $l^2$ of stored
   linear forms — the verifier reconstructs them, so their nonnegativity is unconditional;
4. every claimed polynomial is re-derived from scratch — endpoint polynomials from the witness
   formulas/triples/cut directions, selection vectors from $(a,b)$ and the local coordinates of
   the witness (via the exact frames), disk polynomials from the local coordinates — so the data
   file is *untrusted input*;
5. arcs chain across the period, cross-products are certified positive (arc $<180^\circ$),
   denominators are certified positive;
6. numerical defense-in-depth (optional): random admissible staircases vs. the certified bound.

Machine outputs: `verify_hole_noncover.log`.

## 8. The equality corners

The infimum $F_S\downarrow h$ is *approached but not attained*, in exactly **two** parameter
corners:

* $\mathbf a\to0^+$: then $(s,t)\to(0,0)$; $S\to$ the sector triangle $OV_0V_5$ and the optimal
  orientation tends to $\varphi=90^\circ$. On arc $B$ the certified margin is
  $\ge\varepsilon\,s^k(t-s)^m$-type and every witness collapses to the triangle's corners:
  $W_{st1},W_{st3},W_0\to O$, $W_{V0b}\to V_0$, $W_{V5}\to V_5$, $W_{dip}\to$ the edge
  midpoint of $[V_0,V_5]$ — the certificates vanish exactly there and nowhere else in the
  admissible interior.
* the mirror corner $\mathbf a\to1^-$ (i.e. $a'\to0^+$ under Lemma 2.2), where $S$ tends to the
  sector triangle $OV_0V_1$ at optimal orientation $\to30^\circ$.

The presence of the second corner is forced by Lemma 2.2 and was confirmed numerically (mirrored
staircases give margins equal to machine precision); the prompt's statement that equality is
approached *only* at $\mathbf a\to0^+$ should be read modulo this mirror symmetry.

*Remark (excluded faces).* The certified margins also degenerate as $t-s\to0$ (all gaps equal,
i.e. $a_0+b_0\to1^+$), which is an excluded boundary face of the standing assumptions; the true
margin of $F_S-h$ stays bounded below along that face (the symmetric configurations have large
holes), so this is an artifact of the reduced two-parameter bound, not of $S$ — consistent with
the prompt's warning that the symmetric locus is not the extremal case.
