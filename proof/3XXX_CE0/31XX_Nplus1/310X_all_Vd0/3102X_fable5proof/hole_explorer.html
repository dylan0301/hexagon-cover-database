the reduced hole S̃(s,t) and its triangular support sum

Hexagon minus one sliver K(t,1−s) at V₀ and five petals K(s,1−t) at V₁…V₅.  Fitting functional F(θ) = h(n₀)+h(n₁)+h(n₂), normals n_k = u(θ+120°k); the hole fits in a unit triangle iff F ≤ h = √3⁄2 ≈ 0.8660. The theorem: it never does.

Parameters

s = a₅ (smallest edge split), t = a₀ (largest). t is clamped to (s, tmax(s)] where R₀² = t²+t(1−s)+(1−s)² ≤ 1. Sliders map through the admissible range.

Supports come from arc-length-refined boundary traces of the six sets (accuracy a few ×10⁻⁴), so tight corner margins of a few thousandths display correctly; the amber fill is raster-sampled and cosmetic.
